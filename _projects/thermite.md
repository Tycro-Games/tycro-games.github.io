> **Note:** This project is still in development — this page covers the first 16 weeks of work.

## Overview

**Voxmos** (name is still a work in progress) is a FPS mining game that features raytracing and voxel mining with destruction made with the **Thermite** engine. The engine was made from the beginning of the project build in parallel with the design of the game.

<video controls src="../assets/media/thermite/trailer-block-c.mp4" title="Title"></video>

**Key Achievement:** collaboration with other students on a bigger project(6 months) and publish it on Steam. My biggest accomplishment was getting to know others and working with them while having fun and even learning programming in other domains than engine programming.

---

## What I did

I was a bit more like a generalist on this project, I always tried to do the things that I believed were the most pressing for the success of the project, based on what the team prioritized:

- **Editor additions and improvements:** console with commands, filtering and logs; build packager; physics layer editor (inspired by Godot);
- **CI/CD pipeline:** hybrid setup using Perforce and Github; non-programmers were using Perforce to handle assets, while all the code was developed and checked via various actions on Github;
- **Input System:** via SDL3 support for controller and keyboard + mouse, using input actions;
- **Gameplay mechanics:** mining via rays, early player collision; barge logic for movement and collection of ores; weapon handling state for the 3 types there are in the game.

## Reflection

I would like to emphasize that at the beginning of the project I had to overcome two complex issues that I personally had. The first was that the idea chosen from the concepting phase did not really appeal to me. I did not found it particularly interesting at the time and the thought that I had to work on it for about 6 months was worrying me. The second also related very strongly with the first, because I realized that most of the team was already on the same page right in the first days. I later learned that they were already formed a majority group before even starting the project officially, since they have worked on projects together previously.

This made me feel that my ideas were quite useless to the team very often and I doubted myself a lot at that point in time.

Fortunately, these two problems that caused me a lot of distress in the first months were resolved completely. I started to fit in the group and contribute to discussions about gameplay, pipeline, modules and even game design. The animosity that was mainly in my head in the beginning was overcome by talking with my colleagues and getting to know their characters and many talents. On the first issue, I managed to find interesting to work for this project and I am happy to say that I feel I happy I was able to overcome my initial worry.

In the end, I learned a few new things related to programming and got to meet and work with talented developers on an impressive project. The thing that I will take with me is that my initial feeling and assumptions towards anything can be mended, and interesting things to learn and satisfy's one curiosity can arise from any project.
